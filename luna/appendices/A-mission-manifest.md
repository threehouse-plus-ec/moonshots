## N1 Programme (Crewed Lunar Landing)

The N1 was the Soviet super-heavy launch vehicle intended to compete with Saturn V for crewed lunar landing.

| Launch | Date | Outcome | Notes |
|--------|------|---------|-------|
| N1 3L | 3 Feb 1969 | Failure | First stage engine shutdown; destroyed launch pad |
| N1 4L | 3 Jul 1969 | Failure | First stage explosion; largest non-nuclear explosion in history |
| N1 5L | 26 Jun 1971 | Failure | First stage structural failure |
| N1 6L | 23 Nov 1972 | Failure | First stage explosion |
| N1 7L | — | Cancelled | Programme cancelled after fourth failure |

**Programme Cancellation:** The N1 programme was cancelled in 1974 following four consecutive launch failures. The Soviet crewed lunar landing programme (L3 complex with LOK orbiter and LK lander) was never realised [Source: Siddiqi, *Challenge to Apollo*].

## Luna 25 Failure Analysis

The loss of Luna 25 on 19 August 2023 was traced to a cascading sequence of failures originating in the onboard angular velocity measurement system:

1. **Primary cause:** The onboard computer failed to activate the BIUS-L accelerometer unit (angular velocity sensor) prior to the orbital correction manoeuvre.
2. **Data propagation:** With BIUS-L inactive, the guidance system received zero angular velocity data throughout the burn.
3. **Engine over-burn:** The correction engine fired for 127 seconds instead of the planned 84 seconds, imparting an unrecoverable trajectory deviation.
4. **Impact:** The spacecraft struck the inner rim of Pontecoulant G crater, approximately 400 km from the intended landing site near Boguslavsky crater.
5. **Supply chain context:** The BIUS-L unit was a domestically produced replacement for a previously imported component, substituted as a consequence of post-2014 sanctions on Russia's space sector.

The failure highlighted the systemic risks of component import substitution under sanctions pressure, particularly for safety-critical avionics in planetary missions [Source: Roscosmos investigation commission report; NASA].
