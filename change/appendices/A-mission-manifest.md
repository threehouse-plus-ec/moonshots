# Appendix 4A: Chinese Chang'e Programme Mission Manifest

**Programme:** Chinese Lunar Exploration Program (CLEP) (2007–present)  
**Document Type:** Mission Manifest  
**Date Compiled:** 2026-04-04  
**Source Priority:** CNSA official documents, Li Chunlai et al. (2019) in *Science*, NSSDCA mission pages

---

## Complete Mission Table

| Mission | Phase | Launch Date | Launch Vehicle | Type | Primary Objective | Outcome | Key Notes |
|---------|-------|-------------|----------------|------|-------------------|---------|-----------|
| Chang'e 1 | Phase 1 (Orbit) | 24 Oct 2007 | Long March 3A | Orbiter | First Chinese lunar orbiter; 3D mapping | Success | Entered lunar orbit 7 Nov 2007; impacted Moon 1 Mar 2009 [Source: CNSA] |
| Chang'e 2 | Phase 1 (Orbit) | 1 Oct 2010 | Long March 3C | Orbiter | High-resolution mapping; landing site selection | Success | 1.5m resolution images; departed Moon for L2 8 Jun 2011; flew by asteroid Toutatis Dec 2012 [Source: CNSA] |
| Chang'e 3 | Phase 2 (Landing) | 1 Dec 2013 | Long March 3B | Lander + Rover | First Chinese lunar landing; Yutu rover deployment | Success | Landed 14 Dec 2013 Mare Imbrium; first landing since Luna 24 (1976); Yutu operated 31 months [Source: CNSA] |
| Chang'e 5-T1 | Phase 2–3 Test | 23 Oct 2014 | Long March 3C | Test vehicle | Test return capsule re-entry from lunar distance | Success | Return capsule landed 31 Oct 2014; validated heat shield [Source: CNSA] |
| Chang'e 4 | Phase 2 (Landing) | 8 Dec 2018 | Long March 3B | Lander + Rover | First far-side landing; Yutu-2 rover | Success | Landed 3 Jan 2019 Von Kármán crater; first far-side landing; Queqiao relay used [Source: CNSA] |
| Chang'e 5 | Phase 3 (Sample Return) | 23 Nov 2020 | Long March 5 | Sample return | First lunar sample return since Luna 24 | Success | Landed 1 Dec 2020; returned 1,731g samples 16 Dec 2020; first robotic sample return since 1976 [Source: CNSA] |
| Chang'e 6 | Phase 3 (Sample Return) | 3 May 2024 | Long March 5 | Sample return | First far-side sample return | Success | Landed 1 Jun 2024 Apollo crater (far side); returned 1,935.3 g samples 25 Jun 2024 [Source: CNSA] |
| Chang'e 7 | Phase 4 (Research Station) | 2026 (planned) | Long March 5 | Lander + Rover + Orbiter | South pole exploration; water ice detection | Planned | Target: Shackleton crater region; will search for water ice [Source: CNSA] |
| Chang'e 8 | Phase 4 (Research Station) | 2028 (planned) | Long March 5 | Lander + Rover | In-situ resource utilisation; technology tests | Planned | Will test 3D printing with lunar regolith; international cooperation [Source: CNSA] |

## Programme Phase Structure

| Phase | Period | Objectives | Missions |
|-------|--------|------------|----------|
| **Phase 1: Orbiting** | 2007–2010 | Comprehensive lunar imaging; 3D mapping; resource survey | Chang'e 1, Chang'e 2 |
| **Phase 2: Landing** | 2013–2019 | Soft landing; surface exploration; far-side exploration | Chang'e 3, Chang'e 4 |
| **Phase 3: Sample Return** | 2014–2024 | Robotic sample return; near-side and far-side samples | Chang'e 5-T1, Chang'e 5, Chang'e 6 |
| **Phase 4: Research Station** | 2026–2035+ | South pole exploration; water ice detection; ISRU; build ILRS | Chang'e 7, Chang'e 8, follow-on missions |

## Key Achievements by Mission

### Chang'e 1 (2007–2009)

| Achievement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **First Chinese lunar probe** | Maiden mission to Moon |
| **3D lunar map** | Complete map at 120m resolution |
| **Elemental abundance** | Survey of lunar surface composition |
| **Controlled impact** | Intentional impact on 1 Mar 2009 |

### Chang'e 2 (2010–2012)

| Achievement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **High-resolution imaging** | 1.5m resolution of landing sites |
| **Extended mission** | Departed Moon for Earth-Sun L2 |
| **Asteroid flyby** | Flew within 3.2 km of Toutatis (Dec 2012) |
| **Deep space record** | 70 million km from Earth at farthest point |

### Chang'e 3 (2013–2016)

| Achievement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **First Chinese lunar landing** | First soft landing since Luna 24 (1976) |
| **Yutu rover** | First Chinese rover on another world |
| **Longevity record** | Yutu operated 31 months (planned: 3 months) |
| **Scientific data** | Ground-penetrating radar; surface composition |

### Chang'e 4 (2018–present)

| Achievement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **First far-side landing** | First spacecraft to land on far side |
| **Yutu-2 rover** | Longest-operating lunar rover (still active as of 2026) |
| **Low-frequency radio** | First radio astronomy from lunar far side |
| **Surface discoveries** | Subsurface structure; mantle material evidence |

### Chang'e 5 (2020)

| Achievement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Sample return** | First lunar samples since Luna 24 (1976) |
| **Complex mission** | Orbiter, lander, ascender, returner, trackercraft |
| **Youngest samples** | ~1.7 billion years old (youngest returned) |
| **Drill depth** | Up to 2 metres |

### Chang'e 6 (2024)

| Achievement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **First far-side samples** | First samples from lunar far side |
| **International cooperation** | Payloads from France, Italy, Sweden, Pakistan |
| **Sample mass** | 1,935.3 grams |
| **Mini-rover** | Jinchan rover for surface imaging |
