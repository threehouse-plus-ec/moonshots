# Appendix 1D: Apollo Programme Timeline

**Programme:** Apollo (1961–1972)  
**Document Type:** Programme Timeline  
**Date Compiled:** 2026-04-04

---

## Chronological Milestones

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 25 May 1961 | Kennedy Address to Congress | President Kennedy declares national goal: "landing a man on the Moon and returning him safely to the Earth" before decade's end [Source: JFK Presidential Library] |
| 5–6 Jun 1961 | Apollo Program Initiation | NASA approves lunar landing programme; initial feasibility studies begin [Source: NASA] |
| 11 Jul 1962 | Lunar Orbit Rendezvous (LOR) Decision | NASA selects LOR as primary mission mode over direct ascent and Earth orbit rendezvous [Source: NASA SP-4205] |
| 7 Nov 1962 | Saturn V Approved | NASA announces decision to proceed with Saturn C-5 (later Saturn V) development [Source: NASA] |
| 1 Sep 1963 | Mueller Appointment | George E. Mueller becomes Associate Administrator for Manned Space Flight; introduces five-box management structure [Source: NASA SP-4102] |
| Oct–Nov 1963 | All-Up Testing Decision | Mueller approves all-up testing philosophy for Saturn V; replaces incremental stage testing [Source: NASA SP-4205] |
| 27 Jan 1967 | Apollo 1 Fire | Fire during pre-launch test kills astronauts Grissom, White, and Chaffee; triggers major programme reorganisation [Source: Apollo 204 Review Board] |
| 9 Apr 1967 | Apollo 204 Review Board Report | Review Board publishes findings; recommends extensive safety and design changes [Source: Apollo 204 Review Board] |
| 9 Nov 1967 | Apollo 4 Launch | First Saturn V flight; first all-up test; successful demonstration of full vehicle configuration [Source: NASA] |
| 22 Jan 1968 | Apollo 5 Launch | First Lunar Module flight test in Earth orbit [Source: NASA] |
| 4 Apr 1968 | Apollo 6 Launch | Second Saturn V test; experiences pogo oscillation but still certifies vehicle for crew [Source: NASA] |
| 11 Oct 1968 | Apollo 7 Launch | First crewed Apollo flight; Earth orbit CSM test; crewed by Schirra, Eisele, Cunningham [Source: NASA] |
| 21 Dec 1968 | Apollo 8 Launch | First crewed flight to Moon; first lunar orbit; Borman, Lovell, Anders complete 10 lunar orbits [Source: NASA] |
| 3 Mar 1969 | Apollo 9 Launch | First crewed LM flight; Earth orbit CSM-LM operations test [Source: NASA] |
| 18 May 1969 | Apollo 10 Launch | Dress rehearsal for landing; LM descends to 15.6 km above lunar surface [Source: NASA] |
| 16 Jul 1969 | Apollo 11 Launch | First lunar landing; Armstrong and Aldrin land at Sea of Tranquility; "one small step" [Source: NASA] |
| 20 Jul 1969 | First Moon Walk | Armstrong and Aldrin conduct 2h 31m EVA; collect 21.55 kg samples [Source: NASA] |
| 24 Jul 1969 | Apollo 11 Splashdown | Crew returns safely to Earth; primary Apollo goal achieved [Source: NASA] |
| 14 Nov 1969 | Apollo 12 Launch | Second lunar landing; precision landing near Surveyor 3 [Source: NASA] |
| 11 Apr 1970 | Apollo 13 Launch | Third landing attempt; oxygen tank explosion aborts mission; crew uses LM as lifeboat [Source: Apollo 13 Review Board] |
| 17 Apr 1970 | Apollo 13 Safe Return | Crew splashes down safely; mission termed "successful failure" [Source: NASA] |
| 31 Jan 1971 | Apollo 14 Launch | Third successful lunar landing; Fra Mauro formation [Source: NASA] |
| 26 Jul 1971 | Apollo 15 Launch | First J-mission; first LRV use; extended surface exploration at Hadley-Apennine [Source: NASA] |
| 16 Apr 1972 | Apollo 16 Launch | Fifth lunar landing; Descartes Highlands; first highlands exploration [Source: NASA] |
| 7 Dec 1972 | Apollo 17 Launch | Final Apollo lunar mission; only night launch; Taurus-Littrow valley; geologist Harrison Schmitt [Source: NASA] |
| 19 Dec 1972 | Apollo 17 Splashdown | Final Apollo lunar landing mission concludes [Source: NASA] |
| 2 Jan 1973 | Apollo 18–20 Cancellation | NASA announces cancellation of Apollo 18, 19, and 20 due to budget cuts [Source: NASA] |
| 30 Jun 1975 | Apollo-Soyuz Test Project | Final Apollo spacecraft flight; international docking with Soviet Soyuz [Source: NASA] |

## Programme Phase Summary

| Phase | Period | Key Activities |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Programme Initiation** | 1961–1962 | Kennedy announcement, mode selection, contractor selection |
| **Development** | 1963–1966 | Hardware design and fabrication, testing, crew training |
| **Crisis and Recovery** | 1967 | Apollo 1 fire, investigation, redesign, safety reforms |
| **Flight Qualification** | 1967–1968 | Uncrewed Saturn V tests, Apollo 7, Apollo 8 decision |
| **Lunar Landing Campaign** | 1969–1970 | Apollo 11–14; primary landing objectives |
| **Extended Exploration** | 1971–1972 | Apollo 15–17; J-missions with LRV and extended science |
| **Programme Conclusion** | 1972–1975 | Mission cancellations, Skylab transition, Apollo-Soyuz |
