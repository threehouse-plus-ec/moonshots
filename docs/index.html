Dossier programmes

Five programmes, two tiers of analytical depth.

Full dossiers carry complete appendices with 10–16 ledger entries. Comparative dossiers focus on the systems-engineering signature that makes each programme analytically distinct, with 4–8 entries each.

01

Apollo (1961–1972)

Reference system and success archetype. 16 ledger entries covering crewed lunar exploration, Mueller's five-box management structure, and the systems engineering that delivered six landings.

02

Artemis (2017–present)

Open system, in progress. 6+ provisional entries with time-indexed programme-state claims. Distributed architecture with commercial integration under active evaluation.

03

Luna (1959–1976)

Competing architecture and counterfactual. 5 entries examining the Soviet exploration-first approach with distributed, opaque, and internally competitive error-correction.

04

Chang'e (2007–present)

Incremental control system. 6 entries analysing China's gated phased validation strategy for staged integration across orbiter, lander, rover, and sample return.

05

Chandrayaan (2008–present)

Constrained adaptive system. 5 entries examining failure-based design under severe resource constraint. ISRO's iterative approach turned Chandrayaan-2's partial failure into Chandrayaan-3's success.

Epistemic architecture

Three layers keep measurement separate from interpretation.

The repository enforces strict separation between forensic measurement, interpretive synthesis, and factual reference material. Contamination across layers is a structural violation.

Structural invariant

The Ledger measures, it does not govern. Interpretive synthesis, historical narrative, and comparative analysis are strictly confined to the Sails layer and must not contaminate Ledger entries. Entries terminate at classification. Commentary belongs in Sails or Council Notes.
